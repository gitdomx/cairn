## Character Creation

### Background
- roll or choose from [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-background\|list of backgrounds]]
- choose a name
- add items to [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Inventory\|inventory]]
- roll on tables within backgrounds

### Numbers
1. roll for [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Attributes\|attributes]] and HP (`dice:1d6`)
2. roll for [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-traits\|traits]] and [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-bonds#Bonds Table\|bonds]]
3. roll for age - `dice: 2d20+10`
4. roll for [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-omens\|omen]] (one person in the party)
	- this is read aloud to everyone

#### Optional
- 7-11 Attributes
	- start with 7 on each attribute (STR, DEX, WIL)
	- add from a pool of 11 to each however you see fit
- Forgiving HP Start
	- HP is `dice: 2d4kh1+1`
	- lowered max, consistent start

### Inventory
- 10 inventory slots
	- inventory slots on mounts can be added
- 4 items comfortable carried (in use) on person
- players start with backpack with 6 slots
	- slots are abstract

## Core Rules

### Attributes
- roll `dice: 3d6`for each stat
- **Strength** (STR) - physical power, resisting poison
- **Dexterity** (DEX) - speed, poise
- **Willpower** (WIL) - persuasion, manipulating spells

### Saves
- used to avoid negative outcomes
- characters roll a d20
	- rolls equal to OR under attribute is a pass
	- 1 is always a success
	- 20 is always a failure
- if multiple must make save
	- whoever most at risk rolls (usually the one with lowest attribute)

### Healing & Recovery
- recover HP by:
	- resting for minutes
- after [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Critical Damage\|critical damage]]
	- bandages can stabilise

### Deprivation & Fatigue

#### Deprived
- being deprived:
	- lacking crucial need (e.g. food, sleep, rest)
	- cannot recover HP, attributes, or item slots

#### Fatigue
- **fatigue**
	- added after one day if being deprived
	- may also be added after casting a spell
	- each will occupy one [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Inventory\|inventory]] slot and will last until character recovers
- if no free inventory slot
	- drop an item

### Hirelings
- choose role from Hirelings table in marketplace
- roll `dice:3d6` for each attribute
- roll `dice:1d6` for HP
- give them equipment
- roll character traits to complete character
- alternative option - create character

## Combat Rules
### Rounds
- roughly 10 seconds of real time
- first round order
	1. players who can act
	2. enemies act
- players make DEX save to see if they can act in first round
- subsequent rounds
	1. players act
	2. enemies act

### Actions
- players can
	- move up to 40 ft AND
	- take one action
- actions can be
	- to move again
	- casting a spell
	- attacking
	- a reasonable act
- declare actions taking before making roll
- GM ask for roll if players attempt something risky

### Attacks & Damage
- attacks hit
- roll weapon die and subtract target's [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Armor\|armor]]
	- remaining dealt to target's HP
- if multiple attackers attack same foe
	- roll all weapon die
	- keep highest die as damage
- if attack reduces HP to **exactly zero**
	- player gets [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Scars\|scar]]

### Attack Modifiers
#### Impaired
>[!info] Attacking from a position of weakness makes it impaired. Impaired attacks use 1d4 damage die instead of the normal damage die.

#### Enhanced
>[!info] Attacking from a position of advantage makes it enhanced. Enhanced attacks use 1d12 damage die instead of the normal damage die.

#### Blast
>[!info] Attacks with blast affect all targets in a specified area.

#### Attacking with Two Weapons
>[!info] When using two weapons on the same attack, roll both damage dice and keep the single highest roll as damage. They are denoted with a '+' symbol.
### Armor
- armor provides bonus defense
- subtract armor from damage roll
	- before calculating damage to character
- max of 3 armor (PC or NPC)

### Critical Damage
- **critical damage** is:
	- damage that reduces HP below zero OR
	- damage received while HP is zero
- damage when HP is zero or excess damage:
	- reduces from STR
- when HP is zero after or when taking damage:
	- make STR save using new STR score
	- keep fighting if success

### Ranged Attacks
- ranged attacks
	- can target enemies near enough to see white of eyes
	- against distant targets are [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Impaired\|impaired]]
- ammunition not tracked
	- unless stated otherwise

### Attribute Loss & Outcomes
- STR of zero - death
- DEX of zero - paralyzed
- WIL of zero - delirious
___
- complete loss of DEX or WIL:
	- requires extended rest
	- or extraordinary efforts

## Scars
- if damage to PC reduces HP to exactly zero
	- look up result based on amount taken
- refer to the [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-scars#Scars table\|scars table]]

## Magic

### Spellbooks
- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-spellbooks\|spellbooks]]:
	- contain a single spell
	- take up one slot (inventory)
- spellbooks are rare
	- are not easily transcribed or made
	- may have unusual properties or limitations
- spellbooks are intriguing
	- they draw those who seek arcane power
	- they are considered dangerous to display openly

### Casting Spells
- hold [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Spellbooks\|spellbook]] with both hands
- add [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Fatigue\|fatigue]] to inventory
- can [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Enhanced\|enhance]] spell's impact
	- with additional time
	- no additional cost
- if PC is [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Deprived\|deprived]] or in danger, [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-warden\|warden]] may require [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Saves\|WIL save]]
	- to avoid ill-effects from casting
	- consequences of failure are equal to spell's intended effect, and:
		- could have additional [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Fatigue\|fatigue]]
		- could destroy the spellbook
		- may lead to injury or death

### Scrolls
- **scrolls** are like [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Spellbooks\|spellbooks]], but they are
	- petty
	- do not cause [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Fatigue\|fatigue]]
	- disappear after one use

### Relics
- **relics** are items imbued with a spell or magical power
	- do not cause [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Fatigue\|fatigue]]
	- have limited use
	- have a **recharge** condition

## Dungeon Exploration

### Dungeon Basics
- divided into series of
	- **turns**,
	- **actions**,
	- and **consequences**
- on turn
	- can move distance equal to torch's perimeter (40ft approx)
	- and perform one action
	- action can instead be 3 times movement
		- will increase chance of triggering a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Dungeon Events\|dungeon event]]
- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-warden\|Warden]] should give information about areas and dangers freely
- moving too quickly increases chance for encounters

### Dungeon Exploration Cycle
1. [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-warden\|Warden]] describes surroundings and immediate dangers
2. players declare intended movements and [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Dungeon Actions\|actions]]
3. warden resolves actions simultaneously
4. players record new conditions and resources

### Dungeon Events
- exploring dungeons should be dangerous
- time must be weighed against risk
- Roll on the [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-dungeon-table#Dungeon Table\|dungeon table]] when the party:
	- stays stagnant in a location for more than one dungeon cycle
	- moves quickly without care
	- moves into a new area
	- creates a disturbance

### Dungeon Actions
- actions include many things
	- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Searching\|searching]] for traps
	- forcing open a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Doors\|door]]
	- listening for danger
	- engaging enemy in combat
	- casting a spell
	- dodging a trap
	- running away
	- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Resting\|resting]]
- some have special rules

#### Searching
- can spend a turn exhaustively searching
	- one object or location
	- reveal treasure, traps, secret doors, etc
- larger areas would require more turns
- searching is safe
- adds **time** to exploration
	- possibly increasing danger over time

#### Resting
- spend a turn to restore all HP
- requirements are:
	- a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Light\|light]] source
	- a safe environment
	- no present or oncoming danger
- resting in a dungeon does not restore Fatigue

### Panic
- panic is experienced when:
	- character is surrounded by enemies
	- character is enveloped by darkness
	- character is facing a great fear
- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Saves\|WIL save]] is required to avoid being **panicked**
- panicked characters must make a WIL save as action on their turn
	- to overcome panic
- panicked characters:
	- have zero HP
	- does not act in first round of combat
	- all attacks are impaired

### Dungeon Elements
#### Light
- torches and other sources
	- have 40ft radius of light
	- beyond that will show dim outline
- torches
	- can be lit three (3) times before permanent degradation
- lanterns
	- can be relit indefinitely
	- requires oil can (6 uses)
- characters without light source
	- suffer panic until fixed

#### Doors
- **doors** may be stuck or closed for any reason
- characters can try to open doors
	- through force, with objects, etc
- marching order determines most impacted
	- by entities beyond door
- characters can detect signs of life or danger
	- through careful observation or nearby doors and walls

#### Traps
- cautious characters would have information regarding **traps** in the area
	- allowing them to avoid them
- unwitting characters would trigger traps
	- they may have a 2-in-6 chance
- traps can be detected through carefully searching a room
- damage from traps is taken from [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Attributes\|attributes]] instead of HP
	- usually STR or DEX
	- armor can reduce damage, only if applicable

## Wilderness Exploration

### Watches
- a day is divided into three (3) **watches**:
	- morning
	- afternoon
	- night
- each watch can be 8 hours
- each character can choose one (1) wilderness action per watch
- if characters split
	- each group is treated as independent entities

### Points
- **points** are potential destinations on a map
- may require more than one [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Watches\|watch]] to travel between two points
	- depends on path, terrain, weather, party condition
- party should have rough idea of how to travel between points
	- but not usually the specifics

### Travel Duration
- **travel duration** is counted in [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Watches\|watches]]
- determine time by combining all penalties on the path, including:
	- terrain
	- weather difficulty tables
	- and taking changes to those elements along the route
- for travel via waterways
	- refer to surrounding terrain
- for vast terrain
	- assign maximum of +2 watches
- some instances may allow players to add [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Fatigue\|fatigue]] to improve pace
- consider mounts, guides, and maps to increase travel speed
	- or nullify travel penalties
- refer to [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-path-difficulty#Path Difficulty Tables\|path difficulty table]] for ideas on travel penalties

### Weather
- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-warden\|Warden]] to roll `dice: 1d6` everyday on [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-weather\|weather table]]
- if "Extreme" rolled twice in a row
	- weather turns "Catastrophic"
- keep track of weather rolls

### Wilderness Elements
#### Night
- night travel is slower and more dangerous
- if traveling at night
	- roll twice on wilderness events table
- certain terrain may be easier to travel at night
	- balance changes travel, if needed

#### Sleep
- last [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Watches\|watch]] is usually to [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Make Camp\|make camp]]
- characters need sleep everyday
	- disrupting sleep requires more than a minor interruption
- if party skips Make Camp:
	- add a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Fatigue\|fatigue]]
	- they are [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Deprived\|#Deprived]]
- traveling while sleep-deprived increases [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-path-difficulty#Terrain Difficulty\|terrain difficulty]]

#### Light and the Wilderness
- refer to [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Light\|Light section]]
- light can be blown out by environmental conditions
	- wind, water, dust

### Wilderness Exploration Cycle
1. **Description**
	- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-warden\|Warden]] describes current [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Points\|point]] or region on the map
		- describes how to path, weather, or party condition affects travel speed
	- party plots or adjusts based on description
2. **Wilderness Action**
	- each party member chooses a wilderness action
	- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-warden\|Warden]] narrates outcomes and rolls on the wilderness events table
	- party responds to outcome
3. **Conclusion**
	- players and warden record losses in resources and new conditions
	- cycle repeats

### Wilderness Events
- roll on the [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-wilderness-events#Wilderness Events Table\|wilderness events table]] during [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Wilderness Exploration Cycle\|wilderness exploration]]

### Wilderness Actions

#### Travel
- typically taken by party as one
- party roll `dice: 1d6` to see if get lost along the way
	- risk can change based on [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-path-difficulty\|path difficulty]], maps, party skills, and guides
- if party is lost:
	- spend a wilderness action to recover their way

#### Explore
- one or more party members search a large area for:
	- hidden features
	- scouting ahead
	- treading carefully
- one (1) location or feature is discovered per person who explored
- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#travel\|travel]] action is still required to leave an area
	- unexplored or explored

#### Supply
- one or more party members can hunt, fish, or forage for food
	- collect 1d4 **rations** (3 uses)
	- chances for more bounty increase with additional participants
		- 1d6 for 2 PCs, 1d8 for 3 PCs, etc
		- or 1d4 and additional 2 rations per extra party member (*homebrew*)

#### Make Camp
- party sets up camp
- ration is consumed for:
	- each party member
	- each mount
- **lookout rotation** is needed
	- for undisturbed sleep
- successful rest would allow removal of all [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Fatigue\|fatigue]]

## Downtime
- between game sessions
- players can:
	- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Research\|research]]
		- follow up leads
	- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Training\|improve skills]]
		- sharpen combat skills
		- develop a new skill
	- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Strengthening Ties\|build relationships]]
- players are limited to one (1) **downtime action**
	- must be taken when character is in safe condition
	- character must not be in recovery
	- cannot be performed if it would put character in risk

### Milestones
- **milestones** are for activities requiring multiple steps or long-term investment
- [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-warden\|Warden]] can assign 1-5 milestones to complete a task
	- each milestone is a comprehensive, non-interactive task
	- players may be presented multiple options to complete task
		- with different milestones
- milestones can be altered depending on circumstances of the narrative

### Milestone Costs
- certain milestones have a **cost** in addition to a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Downtime Actions\|downtime action]]
- cost may include:
	- gold (or currency)
	- resources (non-monetary cost)
	- reputation (risking public image)
	- loss (of something specific and unique)
- costs can differ by character skills, connections, etc
- some costs are great
	- may call for a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Saves\|WIL save]], with failure having consequences

### Downtime Actions

#### Research
- PC investigates question about lost lore
- they must have:
	- a formulated question
	- a source of knowledge
		- if they cannot identify a source, spend a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Downtime Actions\|downtime action]] to find one, with no guarantee they will find one

##### Sources
- a **source** holds a partial or complete answer to the PC's [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Research\|research]] question
- they can be:
	- a person, NPC, or PC
	- a faction or group
	- an entity

#### Training
- characters can improve their skills of using an item or ability
- have clear narrative or mechanical improvement through practice or study, such as:
	- dealing more damage with a weapon through sparring
	- being better at navigation in the general area by studying maps
	- learning a language by studying with locals
- players should describe motivation to undergo training
- player should describe how they are going to improve and designate a [[games/cairn/cairn-00-rules/cairn-basics#Training Master\|master]]

##### Training Master
- PCs hoping to train or develop skills need a **master**
- masters can train or help with study
#### Strengthening Ties
- characters can foster a connection with an NPC or faction
- players must
	- identify who they wish to strengthen ties with
	- clarify a specific intent for establishing a bond
		- describe what they hope to achieve from strengthening ties
